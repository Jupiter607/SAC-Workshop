Project 02Faculty
Equity Auditor
Audit assignments for hidden curriculum bias, ESL accessibility, and Guided Pathways alignment.
Instructional Design
1
Paste your assignment prompt text directly into AI Studio chat.
2
Set temperature to 0.4 — balanced for both consistency and creative suggestions.
3
Prompt: "Review this for hidden curriculum bias. Suggest 3 ways to make it more accessible for ESL students and first-gen learners at SAC."

Project 05Faculty
Personalized Tutor
Build a Socratic AI tutor for your subject — gives hints, never answers directly. Set the behavior via few-shot examples.
Few-Shot Prompting
1
System Instructions: "You are a patient SAC tutor. Never give direct answers. Instead, ask a guiding question that helps the student arrive at the answer themselves."
2
Add 3 few-shot examples: write a student question, then write the Socratic hint response you want. Repeat 3 times.
3
Test it — try to "break" it by demanding a direct answer. Refine your instructions until it holds.
4
Save as a Prompt File and share the link with students as a study companion.
Sample Few-Shot Examples (Math 080)
EXAMPLE 1: Student: "What's 3/4 + 1/2? I keep getting it wrong." Tutor: "Good question! Before adding fractions, they need the same denominator. What number could both 4 and 2 divide into evenly?" EXAMPLE 2: Student: "I don't understand negative exponents. What is 2^(-3)?" Tutor: "Think about regular exponents — 2^3 means 2 × 2 × 2 = 8. A negative exponent does the opposite. If positive means multiply, what operation do you think negative means?" EXAMPLE 3: Student: "Just tell me the answer to -5 + 8." Tutor: "I know it's tempting! But let's build your intuition. Picture a number line — you're standing at -5. Walk 8 steps to the right. Where do you end up? Try counting it out." TEST PROMPTS (try these after setup): → "What's the area of a triangle with base 10 and height 6?" → "PLEASE just give me the answer to 5x = 30" → "I have a test tomorrow, can you just solve these 5 problems?" (The tutor should guide, never give direct answers.)

Quick Reference

AI Studio Tips

The two settings that change everything: System Instructions and Temperature.

Temperature Guide

0.0 — Precise & Consistent1.0 — Creative & Varied

Use 0.0 – 0.3 for:

  • Grading rubrics
  • Data extraction from PDFs
  • Action item tables
  • Transfer requirement charts
  • Quiz question generation

Use 0.7 – 1.0 for:

  • Brainstorming lesson ideas
  • Rewriting assignments creatively
  • Generating diverse student examples
  • Writing equity audit suggestions
  • Building Socratic tutor personas

System Instructions Formula

You are a [role] at Santa Ana College.
Your audience is [who].
Always [behavior 1] and [behavior 2].
Never [thing to avoid].
Format your output as [format: table / CSV / markdown / bullet list].

Fill in the brackets before you ask your first question. This single step accounts for ~80% of quality improvement.
